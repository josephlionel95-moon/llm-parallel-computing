# References — Chapter 01: GPU architecture & the roofline

## Foundational

- **Williams, Waterman & Patterson (2009), "Roofline: An Insightful Visual Performance Model for Multicore Architectures", CACM** — the original roofline paper; short, readable, and the organizing lens for this whole course.
- **NVIDIA, CUDA C++ Programming Guide** (docs.nvidia.com/cuda/cuda-c-programming-guide) — chapters on the programming model (grids/blocks/warps), memory hierarchy, and streams; the primary source for every execution-model claim in ch01.
- **NVIDIA, CUDA C++ Best Practices Guide** — the pinned-memory, async-transfer, and occupancy guidance, straight from the vendor.

## Microarchitecture (where honest numbers come from)

- **Jia, Maggioni, Staiger & Scarpazza (2018), "Dissecting the NVIDIA Volta GPU Architecture via Microbenchmarking" (arXiv:1804.06826)** — measured latencies for every cache level; the Turing follow-up (arXiv:1903.07486) covers the T4's generation.
- **Luo et al. (2024), "Benchmarking and Dissecting the Nvidia Hopper GPU Architecture" (arXiv:2402.13499)** — modern remeasurement including Ampere/Hopper comparisons; use it to sanity-check the A100 rows of our table.
- **NVIDIA Tesla T4 datasheet / Turing architecture whitepaper** — spec-sheet peaks used in this chapter (8.1 fp32 TFLOPS, 65 fp16 TFLOPS, 320 GB/s).
- **NVIDIA A100 Tensor Core GPU architecture whitepaper** — 19.5/156/312 TFLOPS, 40 MB L2, 2039 GB/s HBM2e; also the best explanation of tf32.

## Tensor cores & kernels

- **NVIDIA, "Matrix Multiplication Background User's Guide" & "Tensor Core Requirements" (Deep Learning Performance docs)** — the authoritative statement of the multiple-of-8/16 alignment rules and wave quantization.
- **Markidis et al. (2018), "NVIDIA Tensor Core Programmability, Performance & Precision" (arXiv:1803.04014)** — early independent measurement of what tensor cores actually deliver.
- **Volkov (2010), "Better Performance at Lower Occupancy" (GTC talk)** — the classic argument that instruction-level parallelism + register reuse can beat high occupancy; cures occupancy cargo-culting.
- **Volkov (2016), PhD thesis "Understanding Latency Hiding on GPUs"** — Little's-law analysis of GPU throughput, the formal version of our latency-hiding math.

## PyTorch-side

- **PyTorch docs, "CUDA semantics"** (pytorch.org/docs/stable/notes/cuda.html) — streams, async execution, pinned memory, and the caching allocator from the framework's perspective; required reading before ch02.
- **He, Horace — "Making Deep Learning Go Brrrr From First Principles" (2022, horace.io/brrr_intro.html)** — the best informal treatment of compute-bound vs memory-bound vs overhead-bound PyTorch programs; effectively this chapter in blog form.

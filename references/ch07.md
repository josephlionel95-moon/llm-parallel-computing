# Chapter 07 references — collective communication

Annotated bibliography. Order = suggested reading order, not importance.

## Core

1. **Chan, E., Heimlich, M., Purkayastha, A., van de Geijn, R. — "Collective
   communication: theory, practice, and experience" (Concurrency and
   Computation, 2007).**
   The canonical survey: every collective, every algorithm (linear, ring,
   binomial tree, recursive halving/doubling), all in α–β terms. Our chapter
   is a guided tour of its first half with PyTorch code. Read after the
   chapter; keep as a reference forever.

2. **Patarasuk, P., Yuan, X. — "Bandwidth optimal all-reduce algorithms for
   clusters of workstations" (JPDC, 2009).**
   The ring all-reduce optimality proof (our "why 2(p−1)N/p is the floor"
   hand-wave, made rigorous). Short, self-contained, readable with this
   chapter's notation.

3. **Thakur, R., Rabenseifner, R., Gropp, W. — "Optimization of collective
   communication operations in MPICH" (IJHPCA, 2005).**
   How a production library dispatches: which algorithm at which message size
   and process count, with measured crossovers. The intellectual template for
   NCCL's tuner; explains "why does the curve bend there?" better than any
   blog.

## NCCL and practice

4. **NVIDIA — "Massively scale deep learning with NCCL 2.4" (developer blog,
   2019).**
   Double binary trees: why rings stop working at thousands of GPUs (the
   2(p−1)α term) and how two complementary trees keep near-full bandwidth at
   log-p latency. Directly answers exercise 8.

5. **NVIDIA — nccl-tests (github.com/NVIDIA/nccl-tests).**
   The benchmark suite everyone quotes; defines algbw vs busbw (README's
   PERFORMANCE.md is the busbw derivation we teach). Run it on Kaggle after
   `experiments/ch07/benchmark_collectives.py` and compare.

6. **NCCL documentation — "Collective Operations" + "Environment Variables"
   (docs.nvidia.com/deeplearning/nccl).**
   Authoritative semantics; NCCL_ALGO/NCCL_PROTO lets you *force* ring vs tree
   and reproduce the crossover yourself on real GPUs.

## History and applications

7. **Baidu SVAIL — "Bringing HPC techniques to deep learning" (2017, blog;
   code: baidu-research/baidu-allreduce).**
   The post that imported ring all-reduce into deep learning. Historically
   where ch08's gradient averaging meets this chapter's algorithm.

8. **Sergeev, A., Del Balso, M. — "Horovod: fast and easy distributed deep
   learning in TensorFlow" (2018).**
   Ring all-reduce productized, plus tensor fusion (= buckets, foreshadowing
   ch09). Reads in an evening.

9. **Rajbhandari, S. et al. — "ZeRO: Memory Optimizations Toward Training
   Trillion Parameter Models" (SC 2020), §3 only.**
   Why reduce_scatter + all_gather (not all_reduce) is the decomposition that
   unlocks sharding. Skim now as motivation; ch15 studies it properly.

## Background

10. **Hockney, R. — "The communication challenge for MPP" (Parallel Computing,
    1994).**
    Where α–β modeling (as "n½ analysis") comes from. Of historical interest;
    cite it when someone asks where the model originated.

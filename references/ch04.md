# References — Chapter 04: Single-GPU Training Anatomy

## Papers

- **Kingma & Ba (2015), "Adam: A Method for Stochastic Optimization"**
  https://arxiv.org/abs/1412.6980
  Why read: the origin of the m and v buffers whose 8 bytes/param dominate the
  static ledger. Read §2 for the update rule; everything else in ch04's memory
  math follows from those three lines.

- **Micikevicius et al. (2018), "Mixed Precision Training"**
  https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.03740
  Why read: the loss-scaling recipe (scale → backward → unscale → skip on
  inf/nan) exactly as ch04 presents it, plus the histogram (Fig. 3) showing
  real gradient distributions sitting below FP16's representable range —
  the empirical justification for the whole mechanism.

- **Rajbhandari et al. (2020), "ZeRO: Memory Optimizations Toward Training
  Trillion Parameter Models"**
  https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.02054
  Why read: §3.1 formalizes our ledger as 2Ψ + 2Ψ + KΨ with K=12 for
  Adam-mixed-precision. Ch04 derives the numbers; ZeRO decides which GPU holds
  them. Read §3.1 now, the rest in ch15.

- **Kalamkar et al. (2019), "A Study of BFLOAT16 for Deep Learning Training"**
  https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.12322
  Why read: the case that 8 exponent bits (fp32-range) with 7 mantissa bits is
  enough for training without loss scaling — why bf16 replaced fp16 as the
  default on Ampere+.

- **Smith et al. (2022), "Using DeepSpeed and Megatron to Train
  Megatron-Turing NLG 530B"**
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11990
  Why read (skim §5): a production-scale sanity check that the per-parameter
  byte arithmetic from this chapter is what real systems budget against.

## Documentation & code

- **PyTorch AMP docs** — https://pytorch.org/docs/stable/amp.html
  The authoritative description of autocast op-eligibility (which ops run
  fp16 vs fp32) and GradScaler's growth/backoff parameters.

- **PyTorch: CUDA memory management** —
  https://pytorch.org/docs/stable/notes/cuda.html#memory-management
  allocated vs reserved vs nvidia-smi, expandable segments, and
  `torch.cuda.memory._record_memory_history` for exercise 6.

- **torch.profiler recipe** —
  https://pytorch.org/tutorials/recipes/recipes/profiler_recipe.html
  schedule/activities/export_chrome_trace with worked examples; the source of
  ch04's wait/warmup/active pattern.

- **`torch.optim.Adam` source** (search for `state[...]` initialization in
  `_init_group`) — verify with your own eyes that `exp_avg` and `exp_avg_sq`
  are created on the first `step()`, not in the constructor. The lazy-init OOM
  in one function.

## Blog posts / talks

- **Zach DeVito, "A guide to PyTorch's CUDA Caching Allocator"** (pytorch.org
  dev discussions / zdevito.github.io)
  Why read: the allocated/reserved gap, block rounding, and fragmentation —
  the "workspace/cache" account of the ledger, explained by its author.

- **Horace He, "Making Deep Learning Go Brrrr From First Principles"**
  https://horace.io/brrr_intro.html
  Why read: the compute-bound vs memory-bound vs overhead-bound taxonomy that
  ch04's profiler section uses to interpret traces.

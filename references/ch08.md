# Chapter 08 references — data parallelism

Annotated bibliography. Order = suggested reading order.

## Core

1. **Goyal, P. et al. — "Accurate, Large Minibatch SGD: Training ImageNet in
   1 Hour" (2017).**
   The linear LR scaling rule + gradual warmup, with the ablations that made
   large-batch DP respectable. §2 (the rule and its assumption) and §5
   (all-reduce overlap — foreshadows ch09) are the required parts.

2. **Li, S. et al. — "PyTorch Distributed: Experiences on Accelerating Data
   Parallel Training" (VLDB 2020).**
   The DDP paper, but its §2–3 are the best concise statement of DP's
   semantics and costs in the PyTorch idiom. Read §2–3 for this chapter, the
   rest for ch09.

3. **McCandlish, S., Kaplan, J., Amodei, D. et al. — "An Empirical Model of
   Large-Batch Training" (2018).**
   The gradient noise scale: a measurable predictor of the critical batch
   size where DP's returns diminish. The quantitative answer to "how many
   GPUs is too many for pure DP?" — exercise 8 implements its estimator.

## The caveats

4. **Keskar, N. et al. — "On Large-Batch Training for Deep Learning:
   Generalization Gap and Sharp Minima" (ICLR 2017).**
   The classic generalization-gap result; why the batch ceiling is not only
   about optimization speed. Balance against later rebuttals (e.g. Shallue et
   al. below) — the empirical picture is subtler than the headline.

5. **Shallue, C. et al. — "Measuring the Effects of Data Parallelism on
   Neural Network Training" (JMLR 2019).**
   The most careful large-scale study of batch-size scaling: perfect scaling,
   diminishing returns, and maximal useful batch — per task and architecture.
   The empirical companion to McCandlish's theory.

6. **You, Y. et al. — "Large Batch Optimization for Deep Learning: Training
   BERT in 76 minutes" (ICLR 2020).**
   LAMB: layerwise-adaptive LR when linear scaling breaks. What production
   stacks reach for past the Goyal regime.

## Systems context

7. **Dean, J. et al. — "Large Scale Distributed Deep Networks" (NeurIPS
   2012).**
   DistBelief: parameter servers and asynchronous SGD — the design that
   synchronous all-reduce DP displaced. Read to understand *why* (staleness
   vs the determinism/simplicity of the ch08 invariant).

8. **PyTorch docs — `torch.utils.data.DistributedSampler` and
   `torch.nn.parallel.DistributedDataParallel` "basics" note.**
   The official semantics of set_epoch, drop_last, and padding — compare
   against our `shard_indices` line by line.

9. **Smith, S., Kindermans, P.-J., Le, Q. — "Don't Decay the Learning Rate,
   Increase the Batch Size" (ICLR 2018).**
   The batch-size/LR/noise-temperature equivalence from the SGD-as-SDE view;
   the second derivation of linear scaling mentioned in the chapter.

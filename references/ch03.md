# References — Chapter 03: Transformer parameters, activations & FLOPs

## The accounting canon

- **Kaplan et al. (2020), "Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models" (arXiv:2001.08361)** — §2.1 and Appendix derive the N ≈ 12Ld² and C ≈ 6N conventions the entire field uses; our FLOP accounting is theirs.
- **Hoffmann et al. (2022), "Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models" (Chinchilla, arXiv:2203.15556)** — turns C = 6N·D into the ~20 tokens/param rule; needed for exercise 8 and any "how big should my model be" argument.
- **Korthikanti et al. (2022), "Reducing Activation Recomputation in Large Transformer Models" (arXiv:2205.05198)** — §4 is the sbh(34 + 5as/h) activation derivation we adapt to fp32; also the selective-recompute menu previewing ch21.
- **Brown et al. (2020), "Language Models are Few-Shot Learners" (GPT-3, arXiv:2005.14165)** — Table 2.1 supplies the 175B config (L=96, d=12288, a=96) used in our scaling table.
- **Touvron et al. (2023), "Llama 2: Open Foundation and Fine-Tuned Chat Models" (arXiv:2307.09288)** — the 70B config with GQA (8 KV heads); source for the KV-cache comparison row.

## Systems context (where the bills get paid)

- **Narayanan et al. (2021), "Efficient Large-Scale Language Model Training on GPU Clusters Using Megatron-LM" (arXiv:2104.04473)** — applies exactly these formulas to pick 3D-parallel configs on 3072 GPUs; the destination of the course.
- **Rajbhandari et al. (2020), "ZeRO: Memory Optimizations Toward Training Trillion Parameter Models" (arXiv:1910.02054)** — §3's memory analysis (the 16 B/param mixed-precision breakdown) is ch04's ledger; skim it now to see why our N-multiples matter.
- **Pope et al. (2022), "Efficiently Scaling Transformer Inference" (arXiv:2211.05102)** — the rigorous KV-cache and memory-bound-decoding analysis; the inference-side mirror of this chapter.
- **Dao et al. (2022), "FlashAttention" (arXiv:2205.14135)** — kills the as²b activation term by tiling + recomputation; the worked example of ch01 rooflines meeting ch03 accounting.
- **Ainslie et al. (2023), "GQA: Training Generalized Multi-Query Transformer Models" (arXiv:2305.13245)** — why d_kv = a_kv·d/a in modern KV formulas.

## Pedagogical

- **Karpathy, nanoGPT (github.com/karpathy/nanoGPT)** — `model.py`'s param/FLOP estimation methods parallel MiniGPT's; diff them as an exercise.
- **"Transformer Inference Arithmetic" (kipp.ly/transformer-inference-arithmetic, 2022)** — back-of-envelope culture for params/KV/latency; a friendly second pass over this chapter's inference half.
- **EleutherAI, "Transformer Math 101" (blog.eleuther.ai/transformer-math)** — compact restatement of the training-side formulas with worked GPU-count examples; excellent revision sheet against `cheatsheets/memory_and_flops.md`.
